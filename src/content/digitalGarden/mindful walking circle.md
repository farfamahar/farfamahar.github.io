##### Activity 1 (15 \- 30m)  
###### Sound noticing  
“To hear is the physical means that enables perception,” Oliveros continued. “To listen is to give attention to what is perceived, both acoustically and psychologically.” 
\- Pauline Oliveros  
\- You hear with your ears. You listen with your mind  
\- cover their ears with their hands for 20 seconds, and then ask them to remove their  
hands. This action helps them to calibrate and focus on their sense of hearing  
\- We each take turns identifying a sound in our environment until we have exhausted all sounds

- Ask them to listen to the sound that is nearest to them and then to the sound that is farthest from them.  
- Ask them to focus on the sounds they hear with their left ear and then with their right ear  
- Ask them to listen to sounds behind them.  
- Ask if there are any continuous sounds around them.  
- Ask if there are any sounds that are periodic (occur in a pattern) or random.  
- what is the loudest sound you can hear  
- what is the quietest sound you can hear

##### Activity 1 (10:30am)  
**\-** Walk through the park with bird list

The Seven Pleasure of Birding (Birders: The Central Park Effect)  
1\. The beauty of the birds  
2\. The joy of being in a natural setting  
3\. The joy of scientific discovery  
4\. The joys of hunting without the bloodshed  
5\. The joy of puzzle solving  
6\. The joy of collecting  
7\. The unicorn effect (studying a bird before seeing it. It takes on a mythological status. Then seeing it suddenly in the wild and sharing that space with them)

"When I'm birdwatching, I'm not thinking about myself at all. And yet, I think that it's a very intense experience of the self just in relationship to my surroundings. And it's often in those moments that I remember really obvious things, like I'm alive, I will not always be alive. I live on the Earth. These really fundamental things that I think it's easy to go a long time without remembering. So I would say, for me, the most ideal version of doing nothing is something where you can access that feeling as well." \- Jenny Odell
